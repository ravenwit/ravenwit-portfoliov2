The Sum Over Histories

"The probability amplitude $\psi(b, a)$ is the sum over all possible paths..."

$K(x_b, t_b; x_a, t_a) = \int \mathcal{D}[x(t)] e^{\frac{i}{\hbar} S[x(t)]}$

Formal Derivation: The Propagator

1. The Quantum Propagator

In non-relativistic quantum mechanics, the time evolution of a state ket $|\psi(t_a)\rangle$ to $|\psi(t_b)\rangle$ is governed by the unitary operator $U(t_b, t_a) = e^{-i\hat{H}(t_b-t_a)/\hbar}$. The transition amplitude, or propagator, to find a particle at position $x_b$ at time $t_b$ given it started at $x_a$ at $t_a$ is:

$$K(x_b, t_b; x_a, t_a) = \langle x_b | e^{-\frac{i}{\hbar}\hat{H}(t_b - t_a)} | x_a \rangle$$

Feynman's insight was to slice the time interval $T = t_b - t_a$ into $N$ infinitesimal segments $\epsilon = T/N$. By inserting completeness relations $\int dx_i |x_i\rangle\langle x_i| = \mathbb{1}$ at each time step, we derive the path integral formulation.

2. The Action Functional

As $N \to \infty$, the propagator becomes a functional integral over all possible continuous paths $x(t)$ connecting $(x_a, t_a)$ to $(x_b, t_b)$:

$$K(b,a) = \int_{x(t_a)=x_a}^{x(t_b)=x_b} \mathcal{D}[x(t)] \exp\left( \frac{i}{\hbar} S[x(t)] \right)$$

Where $S[x(t)]$ is the classical Action functional:

$$S[x] = \int_{t_a}^{t_b} \mathcal{L}(\dot{x}, x, t) \, dt = \int_{t_a}^{t_b} \left( \frac{1}{2}m\dot{x}^2 - V(x) \right) dt$$

3. Visualization Logic

This visualization renders a Monte Carlo approximation of the integral. We display $N=200$ paths. For each path $j$, we calculate a discretized Action $S_j$.

  • Phase Contribution: Each path contributes a complex number $z_j = e^{i S_j / \hbar}$.
  • Time Evolution: To visualize the wave nature, we multiply by the time-dependent factor $e^{-iEt/\hbar}$, resulting in a rotating phase $\phi_j(t) = \frac{S_j}{\hbar} - \omega t$.
  • The "Blink" (Interference): The opacity of each path on screen corresponds to the real part of this amplitude: $$\text{Opacity}_j \propto \left| \text{Re}\left( e^{i\phi_j(t)} \right) \right|^p$$

4. The Perturbation (Your Cursor)

Your cursor introduces a local external potential $V_{ext}(\mathbf{x})$:

$$S'[x] = \int_{t_a}^{t_b} \left( \mathcal{L}_{free} - V_{ext}(x,t) \right) dt$$

When the cursor approaches a bundle of paths, the local potential $V_{ext}$ increases the Action $S$ significantly. Because $\hbar$ is small in our simulation ($\hbar \approx 0.25$), a change in $S$ causes rapid oscillations in the phase factor $e^{iS/\hbar}$. This leads to destructive interference, visually represented by the paths fading out or "shimmering" rapidly, mimicking the cancellation of non-classical paths in the semi-classical limit ($\hbar \to 0$).
