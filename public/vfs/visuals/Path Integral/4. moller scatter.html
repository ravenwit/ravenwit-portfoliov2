Møller Scattering (e⁻ e⁻ → e⁻ e⁻)

Evaluating the second-order S-Matrix operator for identical electrons exchanging a virtual photon:

$$ \langle f | S^{(2)} | i \rangle \propto \langle 0 | \color{#00ffff}{a_{p_3}} \color{#00ffff}{a_{p_4}} \int d^4x d^4y \, \mathcal{T}\{ \mathcal{H}_{int}(x) \mathcal{H}_{int}(y) \} \color{#00ffff}{a_{p_1}^\dagger} \color{#00ffff}{a_{p_2}^\dagger} | 0 \rangle $$

Because the external particles are identical fermions, the indistinguishability of the states permits both direct ($t$) and exchange ($u$) scattering. However, the annihilation channel ($s$) is strictly forbidden by the vertex algebra.
