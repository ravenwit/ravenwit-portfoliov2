Rigorous Mathematical Framework

I. The Topological Wall and Singularities

We begin with the infinite perturbation series, representing an observable dependent on coupling \( u = g^2 \). The limit as \( N \to \infty \) yields:

$$ Z(u) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-u)^n = 1 - u + u^2 - u^3 + \dots $$

By determining the Lee-Yang zeros of the partial sums, we proved that the roots form an infinitely dense unit circle \( |u|=1 \). This topological wall is created by a strict pole singularity at \( u = -1 \). Any attempt to evaluate the series in the strongly-coupled regime, such as \( u = 4 \), catastrophically diverges because it lies strictly outside the analytic domain.

II. Conformal Mapping (Analytic Continuation)

To evaluate \( Z(4) \), we must perform analytic continuation by warping the complex plane. We introduce a conformal map \( w(u) \) designed to map the singularity exactly to the boundary of a new unit disk, while pulling the target evaluation point strictly inside:

$$ w(u) = \frac{u}{u+2} $$

Under this mapping, the singularity \( u = -1 \implies w(-1) = -1 \). The target point \( u = 4 \implies w(4) = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \). Because \( \left|\frac{2}{3}\right| < 1 \), our target is now safely inside the radius of convergence.

III. Construction of the Homotopy

To visualize the transformation, we construct a continuous topological deformation (a Homotopy) \( H(u, \tau) \) bridging the identity map \( f_0(u) = u \) and the conformal map \( f_1(u) = w(u) \) over the parameter \( \tau \in [0, 1] \):

$$ H(u, \tau) = (1 - \tau)u + \tau \left( \frac{u}{u+2} \right) $$

To compute this in the 3D rendering pipeline, we decouple \( u = x + iy \) into strict real algebraic Cartesian vectors. Rationalizing the denominator of the complex conformal map yields:

$$ X_1(x,y) = \frac{x^2 + 2x + y^2}{(x+2)^2 + y^2}, \quad Y_1(x,y) = \frac{2y}{(x+2)^2 + y^2} $$

The vertex shader position at any frame \( \tau \) is calculated via linear interpolation, augmented with a Gaussian-damped sinusoidal arc through the Z-axis to visually express the spatial bending of the manifold:

$$ X(\tau) = (1-\tau)x + \tau X_1 $$ $$ Y(\tau) = (1-\tau)y + \tau Y_1 $$ $$ Z(\tau) = 2.0 \cdot \sin(\pi \tau) \cdot e^{-0.1(x^2 + y^2)} $$
