Rigorous Deformation Geometry

I. The Complex Surface Manifold

Instead of mapping flat coordinates, we are projecting the absolute physical values of the analytic function \( Z(u) \) into the third dimension. For any complex coordinate \( u = x + iy \), the height (Z-axis) of the manifold is its magnitude:

$$ |Z(u)| = \left| \frac{1}{1 + x + iy} \right| = \frac{1}{\sqrt{(1+x)^2 + y^2}} $$

The color of the surface strictly maps to the complex phase (argument), representing the topological winding of the branch structure:

$$ \arg(Z(u)) = \arctan\left(\frac{-y}{1+x}\right) \in [-\pi, \pi] $$

II. Coordinate Morphing under Homotopy

When the deformation parameter \( \tau \) shifts from \( 0 \) to \( 1 \), the coordinates of the base plane undergo the conformal mapping \( w(u) = \frac{u}{u+2} \). The geometry updates via the linear homotopy:

$$ X(\tau) = (1-\tau)x + \tau \left( \frac{x^2 + 2x + y^2}{(x+2)^2 + y^2} \right) $$ $$ Y(\tau) = (1-\tau)y + \tau \left( \frac{2y}{(x+2)^2 + y^2} \right) $$

III. The Conservation of the Observable

Notice what happens to the Z-axis during this deformation: Nothing.

$$ Z_{height}(\tau) = |Z(u_{initial})| = \text{Constant} $$

The height of each vertex is permanently attached to its original coordinate. As the space stretches, the divergent spire at \( u = -1 \) is physically pushed to the boundary at \( X = -1 \). Simultaneously, the flat, convergent region containing \( u = 4 \) (the target value of \( 0.2 \)) is warped into the center at \( X = 2/3 \). The manifold itself bends to resolve the divergence!
